17 Days Safari, Mountain Trekking & Zanzibar Excursions

An epic adventure combining Kilimanjaro trek, Tanzania safari, and Zanzibar island activities.

Discover Itinerary

Tour Overview

The Lemosho route is traditionally one of the quieter and lesser-known routes on the mountain. It's also the newest and probably the most diverse in terms of the ecological systems and landscapes it passes through. The first two days of this route take you through the lush rainforest before you emerge into heath and moorland then join the Machame Route on the third day. This narrow trail, which begins on the Western side of Kilimanjaro, has some very steep and undulating sections. Hikers overnight in tents.

Detailed Itinerary

Arrival — Kilimanjaro Airport to Moshi

Our friendly transfer driver will meet you at Kilimanjaro airport. After loading the luggage into the vehicle you will make your way to the Chanya Lodge in Moshi. You can relax the rest of the day or go and explore the small town of Moshi. Our team will meet you later in the day for a preliminary briefing in preparation for the days to follow.

Meals: Dinner

Day 1: Londorossi Gate to Big Tree Camp

In the morning you will make your way to the Londorossi gate. After finalizing all registration papers you will continue another 15 km on a bumpy road into the rainforest. Today is the shortest walk of the entire trek, about 2½ hours, giving you a taste of what lies ahead. Your destination is the beautiful Big Tree Camp at 2,780 m.

Altitude: 2,780 m | Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 2: Big Tree Camp to Shira Camp 1

After breakfast you follow a thin trail through the rainforest before leaving the green area behind and heading into the moorland. Erica and Lobelia plants start to take over the landscape. After about 3–4 hours you will reach Shira Camp 1.

Altitude: 3,500 m | Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 3: Shira Camp 1 to Shira Camp 2

Today's trek is less tiring than the day before. You will cross Shira Ridge with great views of the Rift Valley and Mt Meru. You'll also pass the big Senecio plants that can grow to roughly 9 metres in height. Overnight at Shira Camp 2.

Altitude: 3,900 m | Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 4: Shira Camp 2 to Lava Tower to Barranco Camp

You'll climb up to Lava Tower, gaining roughly 740 m in altitude for acclimatization — climb high, sleep low. The Barranco Valley is spectacular with its giant Senecio and Lobelia plants. After 6–7 hours you'll reach Barranco Camp.

Altitude: 3,960 m | Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 5: Barranco Camp to Karanga Camp

After breakfast you tackle the famous Barranco Wall — the steepest section of the route. Once at the top you walk along the mountain ridge with views of the southern glacier. After 3–4 hours you reach Karanga Camp.

Altitude: 4,035 m | Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 6: Karanga Camp to Barafu Camp

A shorter day of 3–4 hours through afro-alpine desert. At Barafu you have an early dinner and rest before the summit attempt.

Altitude: 4,640 m | Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 7: Summit Night — Uhuru Peak to Mweka Camp

Beginning at midnight, a 5–7 hour climb brings you to Stella Point on the crater rim. A further 1–2 hours leads to Uhuru Peak (5,895 m) — the roof of Africa. After celebrating and taking photos, you descend back to Barafu for rest, then continue down to Mweka Camp.

Altitude: 3,080 m | Meals: Breakfast, Dinner

Day 8: Mweka Camp to Mweka Gate to Moshi

After a traditional farewell ceremony from the mountain guides, a 2–3 hour walk through rainforest returns you to Mweka Gate. Your driver takes you back to Chanya Lodge. Relax and take in the excitement of the last few days.

Meals: Breakfast

Day 9: Safari — Moshi to Tarangire National Park

Drive to Tarangire for a full day game drive. The park's permanent river is the lifeline of the area in the dry season, drawing elephants, lions, and enormous herds. Dinner and overnight in Karatu at Farm of Dreams.

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 10: Safari — Ngorongoro Conservation Area

A full day in the Ngorongoro Conservation Area, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The caldera is home to one of Africa's densest concentrations of wildlife. Nearby Laetoli has 3.6-million-year-old hominid footprints. Return to Karatu for the night.

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 11: Safari — Lake Manyara to Zanzibar

After a morning game drive at Lake Manyara — famous for tree-climbing lions and pink flamingos — drive to Kilimanjaro International Airport for your 20:20 flight to Zanzibar.

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 12: Zanzibar — Baraka Turtles Aquarium & Dhow Sunset Cruise

Afternoon visit to Baraka Natural Aquarium to swim with rescued sea turtles, followed by a 90-minute sunset dhow sail from Kendwa Beach (16:30–19:00).

Meals: Lunch, Dinner

Day 13: Zanzibar — Safari Blue Full Day

Depart Fumba village at 09:30 aboard your private traditional dhow for a full day exploring the islands and lagoons around Zanzibar — snorkeling, a white-sand sandbank, and a private seafood BBQ. Return around 16:30.

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 14: Zanzibar — Spice Farm, Stone Town & Jozani Forest

Visit a spice farm (touch, smell, and taste clove, nutmeg, cinnamon, and more), enjoy a traditional Swahili lunch, then explore Prison Island's giant tortoises and Stone Town's UNESCO-listed historic quarter.

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Day 15: Zanzibar — Mnemba Island & Rock Restaurant Dinner

Full morning snorkeling the reefs of Mnemba Atoll — one of Africa's premier dive sites — with the chance to spot dolphins and sea turtles. Evening dinner at the Rock Restaurant, spectacularly set on a rock in the Indian Ocean.

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner

Days 16 & 17: Leisure & Departure

Free time at leisure depending on your flight details. Transfer to Zanzibar airport for your onward journey. End of service.

Meals: Breakfast
